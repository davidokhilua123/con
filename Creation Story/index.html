Poem On "Creation Story" By Emmanuel And David

And God stepped out of space,

And he looked around and said;

I'm Lonely.

I'll make a world,

As God's eyes could go far.

He could only see a vast darkness,

Like a hundred-fold of midnight.

Then God smiled;

And the darkness broke;

And the light stood on his own;

And God said: That's good.

And from the light,

He swung his hands and formed the sun from the light.

He made the sun as a ball and flung it;

Against the darknes,

And configurated the moon and the stars from the darkness,

And God said: That's good.

Then God himself stepped down,

And the sun on his right hand,

And the moon was on his left hand,

The stars were around his head,

And the earth under his feet.

As he walked on the earth;

His feet caused the mountains;

And the valley to sprout up.

Then He looked and saw

That the earth was hot and barren,

And so from his mouth he spat out an ocean body-

And he slapped his hand and lightening came to be-

He stompted his feet and the thunder roared.

Then the green grass sprouted,

And the flowers flourised,

The vine trees pointed their fingers to the sky;

The oak tree spread forth hs branches.

The roots of the the trees and plants spread forth their roots;

And God smiled;

And the rainbow appeared;

And curved round his shoulders;

And God said: That's good.

Then God raised his hand,

And said: Bring Forth! Bring Forth!

And before hands dropped;

The seas covered with fishes;

The forest with beast and fowls

And birds seperate the clouds with their wings,

And God smiled;

And said: That's good.

Then God checked all he created,

The moon, stars, and sun;

And he said: Am lonely still.

He sat at the edge of a mountain,

God thought and thought;

And said: Let me fabricate man in thy image.

The lord who made the sun,

Flung stars far to the corners of the earth,

Foureged the sun from the light,

Moulded the earth into shape with his hands.

God Almighty knelt down and scooped soil from the earth;

And began crafting a masterpiece;

Then he blew the breath of life;

And man became a living soul.

Amen! Amen!
